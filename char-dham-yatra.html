About This Tour

The Char Dham Yatra — a pilgrimage to the four most sacred shrines in Hinduism nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand — is considered one of the most spiritually significant journeys in India. The four dhams are Yamunotri (source of the Yamuna river), Gangotri (source of the Ganga), Kedarnath (one of the 12 Jyotirlingas), and Badrinath (dedicated to Lord Vishnu).

Undertaking this yatra by private chauffeur-driven car means you travel on your own schedule — resting when tired, praying at your own pace, and never rushing through one of India's most breathtaking mountain landscapes. Your driver knows these mountain roads intimately and will ensure your safety, comfort, and timely darshans throughout the 12-day journey.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Arrival at Haridwar / Rishikesh
Day 1

Arrive at Haridwar or Rishikesh — the gateway to Char Dham. Your driver receives you at the railway station or airport. Check in and attend the divine Ganga Aarti at Har-Ki-Pauri (Haridwar) or Triveni Ghat (Rishikesh) in the evening — a profoundly moving ritual that marks the true beginning of your pilgrimage.

Har-Ki-Pauri Ganga Aarti Laxman Jhula
02
Rishikesh → Barkot (Yamunotri Base)
Day 2 · ~230 km

Morning departure toward Barkot, the base for Yamunotri Dham. Enjoy the scenic mountain drive through Chamba, Dharasu, and Barkot. The journey takes you along forested Himalayan roads with stunning river views. Overnight stay at Barkot / Janki Chatti.

Chamba Barkot Janki Chatti
03
Yamunotri Dham Darshan
Day 3 · Trek 6 km

Drive to Janki Chatti (6 km from Yamunotri temple) and trek or take a palanquin/horse to the shrine. Offer prayers at Yamunotri temple and cook rice in the hot Surya Kund spring — a sacred tradition. Return to Barkot by evening.

Yamunotri Temple Surya Kund Divya Shila
04–05
Barkot → Gangotri Dham
Day 4–5 · ~100 km

Drive through Uttarkashi to Gangotri (3,100m altitude), the origin of the sacred Ganga. Visit the Gangotri temple perched above the roaring Bhagirathi river, then trek to Gaumukh glacier (the actual source of Ganga, 18 km trek — optional). The landscape here is breathtakingly raw and majestic.

Gangotri Temple Bhagirathi River Uttarkashi
06–08
Kedarnath Dham
Day 6–8 · ~245 km

Drive to Sonprayag / Gaurikund, then trek 16 km (or helicopter available) to Kedarnath — the highest Jyotirlinga at 3,583m. The ancient stone temple against the backdrop of snow-covered peaks is one of the most awe-inspiring sights in India. Attend the morning and evening aarti. Rest for acclimatisation. Descend on Day 8.

Kedarnath Temple Gaurikund Triyuginarayan
09–11
Badrinath Dham
Day 9–11 · ~210 km

The final and most celebrated dham. Drive through the stunning Panch Prayag confluences — Devprayag, Rudraprayag, Karnaprayag, Nandprayag, and Vishnuprayag — to reach Badrinath (3,133m). Visit the Badrinath Vishnu temple, take a holy dip in Tapt Kund hot spring, and soak in the majestic Neelkanth peak backdrop. Visit Mana village — India's last village before Tibet — and Vasudhara Falls.

Badrinath Temple Mana Village Tapt Kund Panch Prayag
12
Return to Haridwar / Departure
Day 12 · ~310 km

Drive back to Haridwar for your onward train or flight. The sacred Ganga flows alongside for much of the journey — a fitting farewell to a truly transcendent pilgrimage. Arrive Haridwar by afternoon / evening.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the Char Dham Yatra season open?
The Char Dham temples open in late April / early May (on auspicious dates like Akshaya Tritiya) and close by Diwali (October–November) before snowfall. The best time to visit is May–June and September–October. July–August (monsoon) is possible but mountain roads can be affected by landslides.
Is the trek to Kedarnath mandatory or is helicopter available?
The 16 km trek to Kedarnath is the traditional route but is not mandatory. Helicopter services operate from Phata, Sirsi, and Guptkashi to Kedarnath (book in advance, especially in peak season). Ponies and palanquins are also available at Gaurikund for those who cannot trek. We can assist with helicopter bookings.
Do you need to register online for Char Dham Yatra?
Yes, Uttarakhand government mandates online registration for Char Dham Yatra at the official portal (registrationandtouristcare.uk.gov.in). Biometric registration is required at entry points. We assist all our clients with the registration process and documentation required.
What vehicle is suitable for the mountain roads?
For groups up to 6, we recommend the Toyota Innova Crysta or Tata Safari — both are 4WD capable and handle mountain terrain excellently. For larger groups (7–12), the Tempo Traveller is ideal. All our vehicles used on mountain routes are regularly serviced, carry first-aid kits, and our drivers have specific mountain driving certification.
Can senior citizens / physically challenged pilgrims do this yatra?
Absolutely. For elderly or physically challenged pilgrims, we can arrange helicopter services to Kedarnath and Yamunotri, palanquins where trekking is required, and plan rest days to allow acclimatisation at altitude. Badrinath and Gangotri temples are road-accessible, making them easier to visit. We tailor every aspect around your comfort and capability.
