About This Tour

Uttar Pradesh is the spiritual heartland of India — a land where gods walked, where enlightenment was attained, and where the soul of Hindu civilisation burns brightest. This 6-day pilgrimage takes you through its holiest cities: Varanasi, the oldest living city on Earth; Sarnath, where the Buddha gave his first sermon; Prayagraj, where three sacred rivers converge; and Ayodhya, the birthplace of Lord Ram and home to the magnificent new Ram Mandir.

Your private Innova Crysta and verified local chauffeur — who knows the ghats, the temple timings, and the sacred rituals — ensures you experience these places with depth, not just a rushed tick-box. Whether you seek spiritual solace, cultural immersion, or simply want to witness an India that has remained unchanged for millennia, this journey will stay with you forever.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Arrival in Varanasi
Day 1

Your driver receives you at Varanasi Airport or Railway Station. After settling into your hotel, head to Dashashwamedh Ghat for the legendary Ganga Aarti at dusk — a thousand lamps swirling over the sacred river in an ancient ritual that has continued without interruption for centuries. The energy is overwhelming and deeply moving.

Airport / Station Pickup Dashashwamedh Ghat Ganga Aarti (Evening)
02
Varanasi — Ghats & Kashi Vishwanath
Day 2

Before dawn, take a boat ride on the Ganga — the most transcendent experience India offers. Watch the city wake up as sadhus bathe, silk-weavers begin their looms, and smoke rises from the cremation ghats of Manikarnika. Then visit the magnificent new Kashi Vishwanath Corridor and temple, followed by the narrow lanes of the old city with their ancient mandirs and chai stalls.

Dawn Boat Ride on Ganga Kashi Vishwanath Temple Manikarnika Ghat
03
Varanasi → Sarnath
Day 3 · ~10 km

A short morning drive to Sarnath — the Deer Park where the Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. The Dhamek Stupa, one of the most sacred sites in Buddhism, stands 43 metres tall at the exact spot. Visit the Archaeological Museum housing the iconic Lion Capital (original of India's national emblem). Return to Varanasi for evening prayers at Sankat Mochan Temple.

Dhamek Stupa Sarnath Museum Sankat Mochan Temple
04
Varanasi → Prayagraj
Day 4 · ~120 km

Drive west to Prayagraj — the Sangam city, site of the world's largest human gathering (Kumbh Mela). At Triveni Sangam, take a sacred boat ride to the confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati. Visit Allahabad Fort (built by Akbar), the Hanuman Mandir (with its famous reclining Hanuman idol), and Anand Bhavan — the ancestral home of the Nehru-Gandhi family.

Triveni Sangam Boat Ride Allahabad Fort Hanuman Mandir
05
Prayagraj → Ayodhya
Day 5 · ~200 km

Drive to Ayodhya — one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism and the birthplace of Lord Shri Ram. The newly consecrated Ram Mandir is a breathtaking sandstone marvel — its scale and craftsmanship will move you regardless of faith. Visit Kanak Bhawan, Hanuman Garhi, and take an evening walk along the ghats of the Saryu River, beautifully lit at dusk.

Ram Mandir Hanuman Garhi Saryu Ghat (Evening)
06
Ayodhya — Temples & Departure
Day 6

Early morning darshan at Ram Mandir before the crowds arrive. Visit Kanak Bhawan (the golden palace gifted to Sita by Queen Kaikeyi), Ram Ki Paidi ghats, and Nageshwarnath Temple. Afternoon transfer to Lucknow Airport (130 km) or Ayodhya Railway Station for your onward journey — or extend to Mathura-Vrindavan for a complete UP pilgrimage.

Kanak Bhawan Ram Ki Paidi Airport / Station Drop

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Varanasi and Ayodhya?
October to March is the ideal window — pleasant temperatures (12–25°C), clear skies, and all major festivals fall in this period including Diwali (Varanasi's most spectacular), Dev Deepawali (November), and Makar Sankranti. Avoid April–June when temperatures touch 45°C. The monsoon (July–September) sees the Ganga in full flood — dramatic but some ghats get submerged.
Is this a religious-only trip or can non-pilgrims enjoy it?
Absolutely for everyone. Varanasi is one of the most culturally rich cities in the world — a photographer's paradise, a food-lover's dream (chaat, kachori, rabri), and a history enthusiast's treasure. Sarnath is a UNESCO-worthy Buddhist site. Even for secular travellers, the Ganga Aarti and the atmosphere of these ancient cities is profoundly moving.
Is the Ram Mandir open to all visitors?
Yes, the Ram Mandir in Ayodhya is open to all visitors regardless of religion or nationality. Security checks are thorough — no phones, cameras, or electronic items inside the temple premises. We recommend early morning visits (before 8 AM) for shorter queues and a more serene darshan experience. Our driver will guide you through the entry process.
Can we extend to Mathura and Vrindavan?
Yes — a Mathura–Vrindavan extension from Ayodhya (approx 370 km) makes for a complete Uttar Pradesh pilgrimage covering all major Hindu sacred sites. This adds 2 days and completes a circuit through Varanasi, Ayodhya, Mathura, and Vrindavan — visiting the birthplaces of both Lord Ram and Lord Krishna. Ask us for a customised quote.
What are the dress code requirements for temples?
Modest, respectful clothing is required at all temples — covered shoulders and legs for both men and women. You will be required to remove footwear before entering most temples. Carry a dupatta or scarf for head covering at certain shrines. Our driver will brief you on temple etiquette before each visit so you are always prepared.
