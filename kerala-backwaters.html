About This Tour

Kerala — God's Own Country — is India's most serene and lush landscape. Unlike Rajasthan's desert grandeur or the Himalayan heights, Kerala enchants with emerald tea-carpeted hills, misty spice forests, and a 900-km network of serene backwater canals that wind through coconut palms and village life unchanged for centuries.

This 7-day circuit covers the very best of Kerala: begin in Kochi with its Portuguese churches and vibrant Fort Kochi art scene; ascend through tea plantations to Munnar at 1,500m; discover wildlife at Thekkady's Periyar Tiger Reserve; and end with a legendary houseboat cruise on the Alleppey backwaters. All with a Kerala-expert verified driver navigating the hairpin mountain roads with confidence.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Arrival in Kochi — Queen of the Arabian Sea
Day 1

Your Kerala-expert driver receives you at Cochin International Airport. Check in to your Fort Kochi hotel and begin with an atmospheric evening walk through the colonial-era streets — past Dutch cemeteries, Portuguese churches, and galleries. Watch the iconic Chinese fishing nets silhouetted against the Arabian Sea sunset.

Fort Kochi Arrival Chinese Fishing Nets Jew Town (Evening)
02
Kochi — Colonial Heritage & Spice Markets
Day 2 · Full Day

A full day exploring Kochi's layered history. Visit the 16th-century St. Francis Church (where Vasco da Gama was originally buried), the Santa Cruz Basilica, and the Kerala Folklore Museum. Take a heritage walk through Mattancherry Palace and the Paradesi Synagogue in Jew Town. End with fresh seafood on the harbourfront.

St. Francis Church Mattancherry Palace Paradesi Synagogue Spice Market
03
Kochi → Munnar — Tea Country
Day 3 · ~130 km · 4 hrs

The drive to Munnar is one of India's most breathtaking — ascending through rubber plantations and spice gardens before bursting into an endless rolling ocean of emerald tea bushes at 1,500m altitude. Stop at Cheeyappara Waterfalls en route. Arrive in cool, misty Munnar by afternoon. Visit a tea factory and watch the leaves become your morning cup.

Cheeyappara Falls Tea Factory Visit TATA Tea Museum
04
Munnar — Hills & Viewpoints
Day 4 · Full Day

A full day in Munnar's highland paradise. Trek through the Eravikulam National Park (home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr mountain goat), visit the Neelakurinji fields (blooms every 12 years — last bloom 2018, next 2030), and marvel at the Mattupetty Dam and its high-altitude reservoir. A Kathakali cultural performance in the evening completes the day.

Eravikulam Park Mattupetty Dam Echo Point Kathakali Show (Eve)
05
Munnar → Thekkady — Spice & Wildlife
Day 5 · ~100 km · 3 hrs

Drive through cardamom forests and pepper-vine-draped hills to Thekkady, home of the Periyar Tiger Reserve. Take a boat safari on Periyar Lake to spot elephants, bison, and if lucky, tigers coming to drink. Visit a working spice and plantation farm to see cardamom, pepper, cinnamon, and vanilla growing in their natural habitat.

Periyar Boat Safari Spice Plantation Tour Elephant Sanctuary
06
Thekkady → Alleppey — Houseboat Check-In
Day 6 · ~150 km · 4 hrs

Drive through Kerala's lush lowlands to Alleppey (Alappuzha), the "Venice of the East." Check in to your private houseboat (kettuvallam) — a traditional rice barge converted into a floating villa with en-suite cabins, a sundeck, and a private chef. Glide through narrow canals fringed with coconut palms, past village temples and rice paddies. Freshly caught Kerala fish curry served on board.

Houseboat Check-In Backwater Cruise Village Life Views
07
Alleppey → Kochi Departure
Day 7 · ~85 km

Morning on the backwaters — wake to misty canals and the sound of birds. Houseboat checkout by 09:30. Your driver meets you at the boat jetty for a transfer to Cochin International Airport or Ernakulam Railway Station. A gentle, unhurried end to God's Own Country.

Backwater Sunrise Kochi Airport Drop

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Kerala?
September to March is ideal — the post-monsoon greenery is spectacular and weather is pleasant (22–32°C). Munnar is misty and beautiful December–January. The famous Onam festival (August–September) is worth experiencing if your dates align. The monsoon months (June–August) bring heavy rains and some roads can be challenging — though the green landscape is extraordinary.
Is the mountain road to Munnar safe?
The Kochi–Munnar road (NH85) is well-maintained but involves hairpin bends and mountain terrain. Our Kerala drivers are highly experienced on these routes and navigate them daily. The journey itself is scenic and enjoyable — not nerve-wracking. We recommend a morning start so you can enjoy the views in daylight.
Is the houseboat included in the package price?
The base price covers the car and driver only. Houseboat charges (typically ₹7,000–15,000 per night for a 2-bedroom boat with meals included, depending on season and category) are billed separately. We can include the houseboat in a combined quote — just mention it when you enquire and we'll send a complete package price.
Can I add Kovalam Beach or Kanyakumari to this itinerary?
Absolutely. Kovalam Beach (near Thiruvananthapuram) and Kanyakumari (the southernmost tip of India) are popular extensions — roughly 2 additional days. We can also extend to include Varkala's cliff-top beaches or Kumarakom's bird sanctuary. Kerala is endlessly customisable — just tell us your wish list.
Where does the tour start and end?
The standard circuit starts and ends in Kochi (Cochin International Airport). We can also start from Trivandrum airport if your flights are more convenient there. The tour is fully customisable — reversed routing (Alleppey → Munnar → Kochi) is also available depending on your schedule.
